Website Design for Small Business: What You Need Before Building

You Need a Website

80% of customers research online before buying. No website = losing customers to competitors who do have one. But building the right site matters more than building quickly.

Before You Start: Get Clear on Your Goals

What Should Your Site Do?

  • Generate leads (contact form inquiries)?
  • Sell products or services online?
  • Build trust with your brand story?
  • Provide information to existing customers?

Who Are Your Ideal Customers?

Define your target audience. Are they local? Geographic? Age range? Pain points? This shapes what your website should say.

Content Your Site Needs

  • Home Page: Clear headline, one main offering, trust signals
  • Services/Products: What you do and why you're different
  • About Page: Your story, credentials, team
  • Testimonials: Real customer reviews and success stories
  • Contact Page: Multiple ways to reach you (form, phone, email)

Technical Essentials

  • Mobile-Friendly: Works perfectly on phones (60% of traffic)
  • Fast Loading: Pages load in under 3 seconds
  • Easy to Update: You should be able to change content without a developer
  • SSL Security: HTTPS encryption (standard now, required)
  • SEO Ready: Proper titles, descriptions, and heading structure

Budget Reality

Professional small business websites typically cost $2,500-$5,000 for initial build. Expect additional annual costs for hosting (~$100-200/year) and maintenance (~$30-50/month).
